Photogrammetry Digital Characters

1/5

2025 | Agisoft Metashape, Wrap3D, Cinema 4D, Blender, DAZ Studio

A photogrammetry-based system for creating authentic Ghanaian 3D characters. I document volunteers from diverse backgrounds, generating production-ready assets for animation and interactive media.

This process demonstrates the complete pipeline using Delali from Ghana's Ewe tribe as a case study. From 360° photo capture through Metashape processing, retopology, and DAZ Studio integration, each step transforms raw photographic data into an animation-ready character while maintaining cultural authenticity and accurate proportions.

Characters created using photogrammetry foundation with stylistic variations developed in DAZ Studio. This library currently covers several ethnic backgrounds in Ghana. The goal is to create as many characters as possible spanning Ghana's diversity, then expand the methodology across Africa. When animators need authentic African characters, this system provides them.

Contemporary Kente Fashion

2/5

2024-2025 | Marvelous Designer, Cinema 4D, V-Ray

Digital garments exploring contemporary applications of traditional Ghanaian kente textiles. I collaborated with a fashion design student on the wedding gown concept, while developing the other designs independently.

Kente-embellished wedding dress combining ornamental bodice detailing with mixed traditional and modern fabric. Built in Marvelous Designer and rendered on one of my photogrammetry characters to test how the design works on Ghanaian body proportions.

Traditional dashiki kaftan with knotted pattern details and contemporary interpretation with kente patterns. These pieces explore how ceremonial menswear translates into digital garment simulation and investigate new contexts for traditional textiles within modern silhouettes beyond conventional applications.

The Comeback | PowerPoint Animation

3/5

2025 | Microsoft PowerPoint, Freepik Assets

In Ghana where many creators and students lack access to expensive animation software, I explored whether animation principles could be demonstrated using universally available tools. This project tests PowerPoint's animation capabilities for storytelling.

This animated short follows three F1 cars in a race. The lead car breaks down at the start, restarts multiple times, then overtakes both opponents just before the finish line.

Working within PowerPoint's constraints required creative solutions for timing, camera movement, and visual storytelling. This approach reflects how resourcefulness and understanding core principles often matter more than access to industry-standard software.

Akofena

4/5

2025 | Cinema 4D, V-Ray, Blender, After Effects

As look development lead and co-supervisor for Akofena, I established the lighting and shading pipeline that unified six students' work into a cohesive visual language. I created several characters, performed motion reference and voice work for the antagonist, and developed the compositing approach. The project investigated how pre-colonial Ashanti narratives could be visualized through contemporary CG techniques while building reusable assets for our emerging character library.

I performed the physical acting for the protagonist character, using my body mechanics as reference for the animation. This side-by-side comparison shows my live-action performance next to the final animation, which combined auto physics simulation with manual keyframe animation.

This shows my lighting and compositing process. I developed the lighting setup for each character using multiple light passes in V-Ray, then imported these passes into After Effects to composite the final promotional poster.

These are the characters I created and supervised for the project. Supervising this project meant balancing teaching production fundamentals while maintaining visual consistency across six students' contributions through a unified lighting and compositing approach.

Ancestral Warrior

5/5

2025 | Cinema 4D, V-Ray, After Effects

Exploring cultural identity through dramatic character visualization. This piece combines traditional African warrior elements with contemporary digital storytelling techniques.

The lighting design uses practical fire sources and atmospheric volumetrics to create cinematic drama while honoring spiritual significance. Multiple compositing layers build environmental storytelling, connecting heritage to landscape through golden hour lighting that suggests transformation. The look development focused on authentic materials rendered with contemporary production quality - traditional elements presented as modern assertion of cultural strength rather than historical recreation.
